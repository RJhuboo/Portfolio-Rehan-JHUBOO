My project

The MALBOT Project

A collaborative project with Laboratoire Hubert Curien (Lahc), Creatis and Sainbiose at Saint-Etienne. Funded by the EUR MANUTECH SLEIGHT.

Computed Understanding bone ageing is an important problem in rheumatology that concerns not only elderly people but also women going through menopause, and astronautes after space exposure. The disease underlying this is called osteoporosis and is characterised by a decreasing bone mass, a degradation of bone micro-architecture and an increase of porosity. Despite its importance, precise diagnosis of osteoporosis is still an open problem for clinicians as it requires assessing bone’s structure from Computed-Tomography (CT) images that often suffer from poor resolution due to radiation regulation during scanning. Thus, super-resolution (SR) of CT images is required to allow for an accurate diagnosis of osteoporosis. In this talk, we present the specifities of bone SR task, based on a mouse dataset composed 40 low-resolution/high-resolution pairs of mouse’s bone. To this end, we compared three different deep learning-based approaches available in the literature, namely: a convolution neural network (CNN) based SR (FSRCNN), a generative adversarial neural network (GAN) approach (SRGAN) and an image translation-based GAN approach (Pix2pix). Our comparison revealed several interesting findings. First, we observed the inefficiency of commonly used PSNR and SSIM evaluation metrics as the latter didn’t always correlate positively with the biological parameters calculated from images. Second, our study revealed that real degradation of low resolution is different from a bicubic degradation commonly assumed in state-of-the-art on SR, proving the necessity to use real image pairs for training. To provide more biologically coherent SR images, we created a NN which computes biological parameters from images and added it to Pix2pix as a regularization term to guide its optimization. In the future, we plan to extend this work to real-world low-resolution astronauts images acquired at different time steps before and after their journey to space as well as for cadaveric images.

Datasets

For the publication at EUSIPCO 2022, We created our own Computed-Tomography Dataset for super-resolution task. We captured, 41 mice tibias at two resolutions : 19µm and 10.5µm, taken from a scanco µCT. We preprocessed those two resolutions in order to get image pairs as explain in the paper. We want to make this dataset available to any researcher or student who works on CT super-resolution or other subjects. Please contact me, If you are interested by the dataset and wish to get it.
